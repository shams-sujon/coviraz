Coronavirus Symptoms
  • The most common symptoms are fever, a new or worsening cough, and new trouble breathing.
  • Less common symptoms: being very tired, body aches, diarrhea, headache, sore throat, losing your sense of taste or smell.
  • If you work in a hospital or medical office, nursing home or in any health care capacity, contact your health provider and employer immediately if you develop any symptoms of illness. You should not work sick.
How to Protect from COVID-19
  • Stay home if you can, unless you need medical care.
  • Avoid close contact with other people.
  • Clean your hands (for at least 20 seconds) or use at least 60 percent alcohol with a hand sanitizer.
  • Throw used tissues away as you use them.
  • Cover your coughs and sneezes.
  • Speak with your doctor for guidance about testing, alerting the health department, and for when it is safe to leave your home and be around others.
How Coronavirus Spread
  • When people are in near touch the virus will move from person to person. Near touch requires a gap of over 6 feet (2 metres).
  • It can go from person to person in the air when someone who has the virus coughs or sneezes.
  • This will go from person to person because someone who has a infection is touching the air that other people are touching.
  • Those with no symptoms can also have the virus and spread it to other people.
